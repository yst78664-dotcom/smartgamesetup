## Cable Management Strategy: Where to Start

If you're staring at a cable mess and don't know where to begin, here's our recommended approach:

### Step 1: Unplug Everything
Yes, everything. Pull every cable and start fresh. This is also a great time to dust your desk and clean your setup.

### Step 2: Group Cables by Destination
Sort cables into groups: monitor cables, peripheral cables (keyboard, mouse, headset), power cables, and networking cables.

### Step 3: Install Your Under-Desk Tray
Mount your cable tray and place your power strip and surge protector inside it. Route power cables up from the tray to each device.

### Step 4: Bundle Cable Groups
Use velcro ties or neoprene sleeves to bundle each cable group. Route bundles along desk edges using adhesive clips.

### Step 5: Hide the Wall Run
Use cable raceways or cord covers to hide the run from your desk to the wall outlet.

### Step 6: Label Everything
Trust us — future you will thank present you when you need to swap one cable in a bundle of ten.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the best cable management solution for a standing desk?

For standing desks, you need solutions that accommodate movement. A cable management spine (flexible vertebra-style chain) is ideal — it flexes as the desk moves up and down. Avoid rigid raceways or fixed-length cable sleeves that could disconnect or strain cables during height adjustment. The Yecaye kit combined with a separate cable spine is the best combo.

### How do I manage cables without drilling into my desk?

Use adhesive-mounted solutions: cable clips stick to desk edges, cable raceways stick to walls, and neoprene sleeves need no mounting at all. The Stageek raceway kit and JOTO cable sleeves are both completely drill-free. For under-desk trays, some models offer clamp-mount options as alternatives to screws.

### Is it worth paying more for a premium cable management kit?

For most gamers, no. The Yecaye 192-piece kit provides everything you need at a fraction of the premium kit prices. Premium kits like Scandinavian Hub offer better aesthetics and packaging, but the functional result is nearly identical. Spend the savings on better peripherals.

### How often should I redo my cable management?

Redo your cable management whenever you make significant hardware changes (new monitor, new PC, new peripherals). For minor changes like swapping a mouse, just use the existing cable routes. If you used velcro ties instead of zip ties, swapping one cable is trivial.

### Should I use zip ties or velcro ties for cable management?

**Always velcro.** Zip ties are permanent — you'll need to cut them every time you want to swap, add, or remove a cable. Velcro ties are reusable, adjustable, and gentler on cables. The only scenario for zip ties is permanent installations you never plan to change.

### How do I manage cables behind a wall-mounted monitor?

Use a combination of cable raceways (from monitor to desk) and adhesive cable clips behind the [monitor arm](/best-monitor-arm-for-dual-monitor-gaming-setup/). Route cables through the [monitor arm](/best-monitor-arm-for-dual-monitor-gaming-setup/)'s built-in cable management channel if it has one. For a cleaner look, install an in-wall cable pass-through plate — though this requires cutting into drywall.
