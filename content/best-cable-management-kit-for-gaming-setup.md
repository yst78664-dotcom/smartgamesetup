## Cable Management Strategy: Where to Start

If you're staring at a cable mess and don't know where to begin:

### Step 1: Unplug Everything
Yes, everything. Pull every cable and start fresh. Great time to dust your desk too.

### Step 2: Group Cables by Destination
Sort into groups: monitor cables, peripherals (keyboard, mouse, headset), power cables, and networking cables.

### Step 3: Install Your Under-Desk Tray
Mount your cable tray and place your power strip inside it. Route power cables up from the tray to each device.

### Step 4: Bundle Cable Groups
Use velcro ties or neoprene sleeves to bundle each group. Route bundles along desk edges using adhesive clips.

### Step 5: Hide the Wall Run
Use cable raceways or cord covers to hide the run from your desk to the wall outlet.

### Step 6: Label Everything
Future you will thank present you when you need to swap one cable in a bundle of ten.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What's the best cable management solution for a standing desk?**
A: A cable management spine (flexible vertebra-style chain) is ideal — it flexes as the desk moves up and down. Avoid rigid raceways or fixed-length sleeves. The Yecaye kit combined with a separate cable spine is the best combo.

**Q: How do I manage cables without drilling into my desk?**
A: Use adhesive-mounted solutions: cable clips, wall raceways, and neoprene sleeves need no drilling. The Stageek raceway kit and JOTO sleeves are completely drill-free.

**Q: Is it worth paying more for a premium cable management kit?**
A: For most gamers, no. The Yecaye 192-piece kit provides everything you need at a fraction of premium prices. Spend the savings on better peripherals.

**Q: Should I use zip ties or velcro ties?**
A: **Always velcro.** Zip ties are permanent — you'll cut them every time you swap a cable. Velcro ties are reusable, adjustable, and gentler on cables.

**Q: How do I manage cables behind a wall-mounted monitor?**
A: Use cable raceways from monitor to desk and adhesive clips behind the [monitor arm](/best-monitor-arm-for-dual-monitor-gaming-setup/). Route cables through the arm's built-in cable management channel if it has one.
