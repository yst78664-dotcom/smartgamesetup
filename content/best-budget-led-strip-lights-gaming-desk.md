## Installation Tips for Gaming Desk LED Strips

1. **Clean the surface thoroughly** with alcohol before applying adhesive
2. **Test the strip before removing all backing** — ensure colors and connection work
3. **Route cables behind/under your desk** for clean cable management
4. **Leave 2-3 inches between strip and wall** for better light diffusion
5. **Use aluminum channels** for professional look and heat dissipation (optional upgrade)
6. **Connect power near your PC** so lights turn on/off with your system

## Gaming-Specific Setup Ideas

**Behind Monitor Setup:**
- Run strip behind monitor in upside-down U shape
- Mount 2-4 inches from wall for bias lighting effect
- Connect to monitor's USB port for auto on/off

**Under Desk Lighting:**
- Mount strips under desk edges pointing down
- Creates floating desk effect
- Use warm white (3000K) to reduce eye strain during long sessions

**Full Desk Outline:**
- Run strips around entire desk perimeter
- Use RGBIC strips for rainbow chase effects
- Position 1 inch under desk edge for indirect lighting

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long do budget LED strips last?**
A: Quality strips (Govee, Kasa) typically last 25,000+ hours. Budget options may dim after 10,000 hours but still provide good value.

**Q: Can LED strips damage my monitor or desk?**
A: No. LED strips generate minimal heat, and modern adhesives won't damage surfaces when removed properly (use heat from hair dryer).

**Q: Do I need a smart hub for these strips?**
A: Most budget strips connect directly to WiFi. Only Thread-based strips (Nanoleaf) need a border router like Apple TV or HomePod.

**Q: Can I cut LED strips to fit my desk?**
A: Yes! All strips have cutting points marked every 1-3 inches. Cut only at marked points to avoid damage.

**Q: What's the difference between RGB and RGBIC?**
A: RGB shows one color across the entire strip. RGBIC can display multiple colors simultaneously, creating gradients and flowing effects.

**Q: Do LED strips use much electricity?**
A: Very little. A 16ft strip at full brightness uses about 15-20 watts — less than most desk lamps.
