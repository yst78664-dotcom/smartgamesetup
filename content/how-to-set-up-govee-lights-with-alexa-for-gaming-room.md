## Step-by-Step Setup Guide: Govee + Alexa Integration

### Phase 1: Set Up Your Govee Lights (10 minutes)

If your Govee lights aren't already set up:

**1. Install Govee Home App**
- Download from App Store/Google Play
- Create account or sign in
- Grant location permissions (needed for WiFi setup)

**2. Add Your Govee Device**
- Power on your LED strips
- Open Govee Home → tap "+" → "Add Device"  
- Select your strip model from the list
- Follow pairing instructions (usually hold power button 3 seconds)

**3. Connect to WiFi**
- Select your 2.4GHz network (5GHz won't work)
- Enter WiFi password
- Wait for "Connection Successful" message
- Test basic controls (on/off, color change)

### Phase 2: Enable Alexa Integration (5 minutes)

**1. Link Govee to Alexa**
- Open Amazon Alexa app
- Tap "More" → "Skills & Games"
- Search for "Govee"
- Tap "Govee" skill → "Enable to Use"
- Sign in with your Govee account credentials

**2. Discover Devices**
- In Alexa app, say "Alexa, discover my devices" OR
- Tap "Devices" → "+" → "Add Device" → "Other"
- Wait 30-45 seconds for discovery
- Your Govee lights should appear with their names

**3. Test Voice Control**
- Say: "Alexa, turn on [device name]"
- Try: "Alexa, set [device name] to blue"
- Test: "Alexa, dim [device name] to 50%"

### Phase 3: Customize Device Names (2 minutes)

**Default names are clunky.** Change them to something natural:

**Instead of:** "Govee RGBIC Strip H6117"  
**Use:** "Gaming lights" or "Desk lights"

**How to rename:**
- Alexa app → "Devices" → select your Govee device
- Tap gear icon → "Edit Name"
- Choose something short and memorable
- Save changes

**Pro naming tips:**
- ✅ "Gaming lights" — Easy to remember
- ✅ "Monitor backlight" — Specific and clear  
- ✅ "Desk strip" — Simple and descriptive
- ❌ "Gaming room LED strip lights" — Too long
- ❌ "Strip 1" — Confusing with multiple devices

## Creating Custom Alexa Routines for Gaming

Routines are where Alexa + Govee really shines. Here are **5 essential gaming routines** every setup should have:

### Routine 1: "Gaming Time" (Morning Setup)

**Trigger:** Voice ("Alexa, gaming time")  
**Actions:**
1. Turn on gaming lights → Set to blue at 80%
2. Turn on gaming PC (smart plug required)
3. Set Echo to "Do Not Disturb" mode
4. Play energetic gaming playlist

**Setup in Alexa app:**

- Phrase: "Gaming time"

**Trigger:** Voice ("Alexa, streaming mode")  
**Actions:**
1. Gaming lights → Bright white at 90%
2. Turn off other room lights (if smart)

**Trigger:** Voice ("Alexa, competitive mode")

**Trigger:** Voice ("Alexa, chill gaming")  
**Actions:**
1. Gaming lights → Purple/pink gradient at 60%
2. Play lo-fi gaming playlist
3. Set comfortable room temperature (if smart thermostat)

**Trigger:** Voice ("Alexa, gaming over") OR Scheduled (bedtime)  
**Actions:**
1. Gradually dim gaming lights to 10%
2. Turn off gaming PC (smart plug)  
3. Set sleep timer for any music
4. Switch to "Good night" lighting

**💡 Pro Tip:** Use gradual dimming in routines. Sudden light changes are jarring during long gaming sessions.

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### "Device Doesn't Respond" Problems

**Issue:** Alexa says "Device isn't responding"  
**Solutions:**
1. Check WiFi connection on both devices
2. Power cycle your Govee lights (unplug 10 seconds)
3. Restart your Echo device  
4. Re-discover devices in Alexa app

### Slow Response Times

**Issue:** 3-5 second delay between command and action  
**Solutions:**
1. Move Echo closer to WiFi router
2. Reduce network congestion (pause downloads/streams)
3. Update Govee Home and Alexa apps
4. Use shorter device names

### Wrong Colors/Brightness

**Issue:** Alexa sets wrong colors or brightness levels  
**Solutions:**
1. Be more specific: "Set to bright red" vs "red"
2. Use percentages: "Set to 75%" instead of "bright"
3. Create custom routines for exact settings
4. Check device name pronunciation

### Multiple Device Confusion

**Issue:** Alexa controls wrong lights  
**Solutions:**
1. Use unique, distinct names for each device
2. Specify room names: "Turn on bedroom gaming lights"
3. Group devices in Alexa app by room
4. Use "nearby" detection for closest device
