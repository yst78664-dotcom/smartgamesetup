## How to Choose the Right Dual Monitor Arm

### Check Monitor Weight and VESA Pattern
Weigh your monitors and verify VESA mounting holes (75×75mm or 100×100mm). Most gaming monitors have them, but some curved ultrawides don't.

### Measure Your Desk Thickness
Clamp mounts fit desks 0.5"-3" thick. Glass or thin desks need a grommet (bolt-through) mount.

### Consider Future Upgrades
Planning to go from 27" to 32"? Buy an arm rated for 32" now — cheaper than replacing later.

### Gas Spring vs Mechanical
Gas springs (most options) are smooth and intuitive. Mechanical (Humanscale) are more precise but costlier. Both work well.

## Installation Tips

1. **Clear your desk completely** before starting
2. **Protect desk surface** with cloth under the clamp pad
3. **Have a second person** hold monitors during mounting
4. **Adjust tension before mounting monitors** — easier with empty arms
5. **Route cables last** — get positions perfect first
6. **Don't over-tighten the clamp** — can crack particle board edges

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can a dual arm hold two different-sized monitors?**
A: Yes. Each arm operates independently — mount a 27" main and 24" secondary without issues. Adjust tension on each arm separately.

**Q: Will a monitor arm damage my desk?**
A: Quality arms include rubber pads on the clamp. Very thin desks, glass, or particle board edges can crack — use a reinforcement plate for protection.

**Q: Do monitor arms work with curved monitors?**
A: Yes, but curves can cause monitors to angle away from each other. Most arms have enough tilt to compensate.

**Q: Dual arm vs two single arms?**
A: Dual arm is cleaner (one clamp) and usually cheaper. Two singles offer more independent positioning — useful for very different-sized monitors.

**Q: Can I mount on an IKEA desk?**
A: Hollow-core IKEA desks (LINNMON) can crack — use a reinforcement plate. Solid wood (KARLBY, GERTON) handles arms fine.
