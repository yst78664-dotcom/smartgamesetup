## Why Gamers Need Smart Plugs

Before diving into reviews, here's why a smart plug makes sense specifically for gaming setups:

### 1. Kill Phantom Power Draw
A gaming PC in sleep mode still draws 5-15W. Your monitor in standby draws 1-3W. RGB controllers, USB hubs, speaker amps — they all sip power 24/7. A smart plug lets you cut all phantom power with a voice command or on a schedule.

### 2. Track Your Actual Power Costs
Ever wondered what your gaming sessions actually cost in electricity? Smart plugs with energy monitoring show you real-time wattage, daily consumption, and monthly cost estimates. Some gamers discover their "idle" setup costs more per month than their active gaming sessions.

### 3. Automate Your Setup
Set schedules to power on your setup before you get home from work and power off at bedtime. Create voice routines: "Alexa, game time" turns on your PC, monitor, and lights. "Alexa, goodnight" kills everything.

### 4. Protect Your Hardware
Some smart plugs can alert you to power anomalies or automatically cut power during voltage spikes, adding a layer of protection beyond your surge protector.

## Smart Plug Power Management Tips for Gamers

### Create a "Game Time" Routine
Set up a voice routine that turns on your gaming PC's smart plug, your monitor plug, and your smart lights simultaneously. One command powers up your entire battlestation.

### Schedule Auto-Off at Bedtime
Set your gaming setup's smart plug to turn off at 1 AM (or whenever your cutoff should be). This kills phantom power and serves as a gentle reminder that it's time to stop gaming.

### Monitor Your Baseline
Use energy monitoring to identify your setup's idle power draw. If it's above 10W with everything "off," you have devices consuming unnecessary phantom power that a smart plug schedule can eliminate.

### Use Separate Plugs for PC and Peripherals
Put your gaming PC on one smart plug and everything else (monitor, speakers, lights) on another. This way you can cut peripheral power while leaving your PC running for downloads or updates.

### Don't Smart-Plug Your PC's UPS
If you use an uninterruptible power supply (UPS), plug the UPS directly into the wall — not through a smart plug. Smart plugs add resistance and can interfere with the UPS's line conditioning. Smart-plug your non-critical peripherals instead.

## Smart Plug Safety for Gaming PCs

A common concern: **Is it safe to use a smart plug with a gaming PC?**

Yes, with caveats:

- **Check your wattage.** A 15A smart plug handles 1800W. Most gaming PCs draw 300-700W under full load. You're well within limits unless you're running a dual-GPU workstation *and* a 4K monitor *and* a space heater through the same plug.
- **Use a surge protector between the plug and your PC.** The smart plug controls power; the surge protector protects against spikes.
- **Don't stack smart plugs.** Never plug a smart plug into another smart plug or into a power strip that's plugged into a smart plug (daisy-chaining).
- **Avoid cutting power during operation.** Use your smart plug for startup and idle-off, not for hard-killing your PC during gameplay. Always shut down your OS first, then let the smart plug handle phantom power afterward.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Will a smart plug damage my gaming PC?

No. A smart plug is just an electronically-controlled on/off switch. It passes through the same power your wall outlet provides. The only risk is cutting power while your PC is running (equivalent to pulling the plug), which can corrupt files. Always shut down your PC through Windows before letting a smart plug cut power.

### How much money can a smart plug actually save me?

Typical gaming setups draw 30-80W in phantom/idle power. At the US average electricity rate (~$0.16/kWh), that's $3-10/month or $36-120/year. A smart plug costs $10-25 and eliminates most or all of this waste. Most gamers recoup the cost within 3-6 months.

### Can I use a smart plug with a power strip / surge protector?

Yes — plug the smart plug into the wall, then plug your surge protector into the smart plug. This is the recommended setup for gaming PCs. The smart plug controls power to the entire strip, and the surge protector handles spike protection.

### Do smart plugs work with gaming PCs that have Wake-on-LAN?

Yes, but with a caveat. Wake-on-LAN requires your PC's network card to receive trickle power from the power supply. If your smart plug cuts power completely, WoL won't work until the plug turns back on. Solution: schedule the smart plug to turn on 5 minutes before your WoL trigger.

### What's the difference between a smart plug and a smart power strip?

A smart plug controls a single outlet (on/off for everything plugged into it). A smart power strip has multiple individually-controllable outlets, so you can turn your monitor off while keeping your PC on. Smart power strips cost more but offer finer control — see our guide on [best smart power strips for gaming desk setup](AFFILIATE_LINK_SMART_POWER_STRIP_ARTICLE).

### Can I control a smart plug when I'm away from home?

Yes. All Wi-Fi smart plugs on this list (except Eve Energy in local-only mode) support remote control through their apps from anywhere with internet. You can turn your setup on/off from work, check if you left it running, or trigger routines remotely.
