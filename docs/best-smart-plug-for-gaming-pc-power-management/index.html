    Smart Plug Tips for Gamers

    Create a "Game Time" Routine

    Voice routine that turns on your PC plug, monitor plug, and LED strip lights simultaneously. Pair with a smart speaker for hands-free control.

    Schedule Auto-Off at Bedtime

    Set plugs to turn off at 1 AM — kills phantom power and reminds you to stop gaming.

    Use Separate Plugs for PC and Peripherals

    PC on one plug, everything else on another. Cut peripheral power while leaving PC running for downloads.

    Safety Notes

    • Check wattage: 15A handles 1800W. Most gaming PCs draw 300-700W — well within limits.
    • Use a surge protector between the plug and your PC.
    • Don't stack smart plugs or daisy-chain them.
    • Don't cut power during operation — always shut down Windows first.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Q: Will a smart plug damage my gaming PC? A: No. It's just an electronically-controlled on/off switch. The only risk is cutting power while running (same as pulling the plug), which can corrupt files. Always shut down first.

    Q: How much money can a smart plug save? A: Typical gaming setups draw 30-80W idle. At ~$0.16/kWh, that's $36-120/year. A $10-25 plug eliminates most of this waste — recouped in 3-6 months.

    Q: Can I use a smart plug with a surge protector? A: Yes — plug the smart plug into the wall, then your surge protector into the smart plug. Recommended setup for gaming PCs.

    Q: Do smart plugs work with Wake-on-LAN? A: WoL needs trickle power from the PSU. If the smart plug cuts power, WoL won't work until the plug turns on. Schedule the plug to turn on 5 minutes before your WoL trigger.

    Q: Smart plug vs smart power strip? A: A plug controls one outlet. A power strip has multiple individually-controllable outlets for finer control. Strips cost more but let you turn your monitor off while keeping your PC on.
