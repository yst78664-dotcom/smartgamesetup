    Build Quality & Design: Different Philosophies

    🎮 Logitech G502 X

    The feature-packed workhorse

    The G502 X is heavyweight at 121g — built for stability and precision rather than speed. This is Logitech's flagship productivity gaming mouse.

    Build quality: Premium construction with metal scroll wheel and solid plastic body. Zero flex or creaking under pressure.

    Design philosophy: Maximum functionality with 13 programmable buttons, dual-mode scroll wheel, and adjustable weight system (though weights not included in this model).

    Grip style: Best for palm and claw grip users who prefer substantial mice.

    The ultra-lightweight speed demon

    The DeathAdder V3 is featherlight at 59g — designed for competitive esports where every millisecond matters.

    Build quality: Surprisingly solid despite the light weight. Smart internal design removes unnecessary material without sacrificing durability.

    Design philosophy: Pure gaming focus with minimal buttons, maximum speed, and the iconic DeathAdder ergonomic shape refined over generations.

    Grip style: Universally comfortable for palm, claw, and fingertip grips due to its refined ergonomic shape.

    Gaming Performance: Where Weight Makes All the Difference

    We tested both mice across multiple game genres for 2 weeks each:

    Competitive FPS (Counter-Strike 2, Valorant, Apex Legends)

    Razer DeathAdder V3:

    • Flick shots: 59g weight allows for lightning-fast directional changes
    • Tracking targets: Effortless smooth tracking without fatigue
    • Long sessions: No arm strain during 4+ hour practice sessions
    • 8K polling rate: Noticeably smoother cursor movement (on supported systems)

    Logitech G502 X:

    • Pixel-perfect precision: 121g weight provides rock-solid stability for micro-adjustments
    • Recoil control: Heavier weight helps maintain consistent spray patterns
    • Sniper button: Instant DPI reduction for long-range shots
    • More fatigue: Noticeable arm strain during extended competitive play

    Winner: Razer DeathAdder V3 (weight advantage is significant in competitive FPS)

    Razer DeathAdder V3:

    • Comfortable for long sessions due to lightweight design
    • Clean button layout reduces accidental clicks
    • Excellent shape for extended gameplay

    Winner: Logitech G502 X (features matter more than weight in slower-paced games)

    Battle Royales (Fortnite, PUBG, Warzone)

    Razer DeathAdder V3:

    Hand Size Compatibility

    Logitech G502 X:

    • Grip styles: Palm grip (excellent), claw grip (good), fingertip (not recommended)
    • Hand position: Fills palm completely, thumb naturally rests on side buttons

    Razer DeathAdder V3:

    • Best for: Small to large hands (16-22cm length)
    • Grip styles: Palm grip (excellent), claw grip (excellent), fingertip (good)
    • Hand position: Refined ergonomic curve fits most hand shapes perfectly

    Long Session Comfort (4+ Hours)

    Weight impact on fatigue:

    • G502 X: Noticeable arm fatigue after 3+ hours of competitive gaming
    • DeathAdder V3: Minimal fatigue even during 6+ hour sessions

    Shape comfort:

    • G502 X: Aggressive thumb shelf can cause pressure points for some users
    • DeathAdder V3: Universally comfortable shape with smooth contours

    Winner: Razer DeathAdder V3 (lighter weight and refined shape reduce fatigue)

    Button Layout & Functionality

    🔘 Logitech G502 X Button Features

    13 programmable buttons:

    • Primary clicks: Left/right with LIGHTFORCE hybrid switches
    • Scroll wheel: Middle click + infinite scroll toggle + tilt left/right
    • Thumb cluster: Forward/back + 2 additional side buttons
    • Index finger: DPI up/down + sniper button
    • G-Shift button: Doubles functionality when held

    Unique features:

    • Infinite scroll wheel: Perfect for long documents and web browsing
    • Sniper button: Instant DPI reduction (great for AWP shots)
    • Dual-mode scroll: Switch between notched and free-spin modes

    🎯 Razer DeathAdder V3 Button Features

    6 programmable buttons:

    • Primary clicks: Left/right with optical Gen-3 switches
    • Scroll wheel: Middle click only (no tilt functions)
    • Thumb buttons: Forward/back navigation
    • DPI button: Single button for cycling through DPI stages

    Design philosophy:

    • Minimalist approach: Only essential buttons to avoid accidental clicks
    • Clean layout: Everything easily reachable without hand repositioning
    • Pro-gamer approved: Preferred button count by esports professionals

    Winner: Depends on use case

    • For gaming: DeathAdder V3 (cleaner, fewer accidental clicks)
    • For productivity: G502 X (13 buttons offer massive customization)

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Q: Is the weight difference really that noticeable?
    A: Absolutely. Going from 121g to 59g is like removing a small smartphone from your mouse. You'll feel the difference immediately.

    Q: Can I add weights to the DeathAdder V3?
    A: No, the V3 doesn't have a weight system. It's designed to be as light as possible.

    Q: Which mouse is better for large hands?
    A: Both work well for large hands, but the G502 X provides more palm support due to its substantial size.

    Q: Does the 8K polling rate on the DeathAdder V3 matter?
    A: For competitive gaming, yes. You'll notice smoother cursor movement, especially on high refresh rate monitors.

    Q: Which has better switches?
    A: Both use optical switches rated for 90 million clicks. The DeathAdder V3 feels slightly more responsive, while the G502 X provides more tactile feedback.
