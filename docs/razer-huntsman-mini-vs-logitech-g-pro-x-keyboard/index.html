    The Quick Verdict: Razer Huntsman Mini vs Logitech G PRO X

    For competitive gaming: Razer Huntsman Mini wins (faster switches, better response time)
    For typing & productivity: Logitech G PRO X wins (better key stability, more switch options)
    For build quality: Tie (both are rock solid)
    For software: Logitech G PRO X wins (G HUB is more mature than Synapse)
    For value: Razer Huntsman Mini wins (typically $30-50 cheaper)

    After 30 days of daily use with both keyboards, the Razer Huntsman Mini is our winner for pure gaming performance, while the Logitech G PRO X is better for mixed use (gaming + typing work).

    Build Quality & Design: Both Are Premium

    🎮 Razer Huntsman Mini

    The ultra-compact champion

    The Huntsman Mini is 60% size — just the essential keys. No F-row, no arrow cluster, no numpad. This saves massive desk space but requires Function key combos for everything else.

    Build quality: Aluminum top plate feels premium. Zero flex during aggressive gaming. The matte black finish resists fingerprints well.

    Keycaps: Doubleshot PBT with nice texture. Legends are crisp and won't fade.

    Cable: USB-C is future-proof and the included braided cable feels quality.

    The esports professional's choice

    The G PRO X is tenkeyless (87-key) — keeps F-row and arrow keys but drops the numpad. Better for productivity than the Huntsman Mini.

    Build quality: Aircraft-grade aluminum frame. This thing is built like a tank. At 980g, it won't slide during intense gaming.

    Keycaps: Standard ABS keycaps feel decent but may develop shine over time. Easy to replace with aftermarket sets.

    Cable: Micro-USB feels dated in 2026, but the cable is detachable and high-quality.

    Switch Performance: Where They Diverge Most

    Razer Linear Optical Switches

    How they work: Light beam actuation (no physical contact point)

    Gaming performance:

    • Actuation: 1.0mm (incredibly fast)
    • Response time: 0.2ms (nearly instant)
    • Feel: Buttery smooth, consistent every press
    • Sound: Quiet linear action with subtle click

    Real-world gaming:

    • Counter-Strike 2: Counter-strafing feels instant
    • Valorant: Amazing for quick peek shots
    • League of Legends: Combo execution is effortless
    • Fortnite: Build battles feel more responsive

    Longevity: Rated for 100 million actuations (vs 50 million for mechanical)

    Logitech GX Mechanical Switches

    How they work: Traditional mechanical contact switches

    Switch options:

    • GX Red (Linear): 50g, 2.0mm actuation
    • GX Brown (Tactile): 70g, 2.0mm actuation
    • GX Blue (Clicky): 50g, 2.0mm actuation

    Gaming performance:

    • Actuation: 2.0mm (standard mechanical distance)
    • Response time: ~1ms (excellent, but not optical-fast)
    • Feel: Traditional mechanical feedback
    • Sound: Varies by switch (Blue is loud, Red is quiet)

    Real-world gaming:

    • Feels familiar if you're coming from other mechanical keyboards
    • More deliberate actuation reduces accidental key presses
    • Better for typing due to traditional travel distance

    Winner for gaming: Razer Huntsman Mini (optical switches are genuinely faster)
    Winner for typing: Logitech G PRO X (traditional feel, more switch variety)

    Gaming Performance: Tested in Real Games

    We tested both keyboards across multiple game genres for 2 weeks each:

    Competitive FPS (Counter-Strike 2, Valorant)

    Razer Huntsman Mini:

    • Strafe response: Instant direction changes
    • Crouch/jump timing: Noticeably faster than mechanical
    • Counter-strafing: Feels more precise
    • ADAD spam: Effortless rhythm

    Logitech G PRO X:

    • More deliberate actuation prevents accidental key presses during tense moments
    • Heavier switches provide better "confirmation" of key presses
    • Arrow keys available for easier bind customization

    Winner: Razer Huntsman Mini (the speed advantage is real in competitive FPS)

    MOBAs (League of Legends, Dota 2)

    Both keyboards excelled here:

    • Combo execution: Both handle rapid key sequences well
    • Spell timing: No noticeable difference in reaction time
    • Item activation: Both are fast enough for pro-level play

    Winner: Tie (both are overkill for MOBA reaction times)

    MMORPGs & Single-Player

    Razer Huntsman Mini drawbacks:

    • No F-keys makes MMO hotbars harder to reach
    • Function layer required for many game functions
    • Arrow keys missing for menu navigation

    Logitech G PRO X advantages:

    • Full F-row perfect for MMO abilities
    • Arrow keys for easy menu navigation
    • More keys available for complex binds

    Winner: Logitech G PRO X (functionality beats speed for complex games)

    Final Verdict: Our Winner

    🥇 For Pure Gaming: Razer Huntsman Mini
    🥈 For Mixed Use: Logitech G PRO X

    The Razer Huntsman Mini wins our comparison for dedicated gamers who prioritize speed above all else. The optical switches genuinely provide a competitive advantage in fast-paced games, and the compact size frees up valuable desk space for mouse movement.

    However, the Logitech G PRO X is the better all-around keyboard for most people. It handles gaming excellently while being much better for productivity tasks, thanks to its traditional layout and reliable software.

    Our recommendation: If you're a competitive FPS player or want the absolute fastest gaming keyboard, get the Huntsman Mini. For everyone else, the G PRO X offers better long-term versatility.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Q: Can I add arrow keys to the Huntsman Mini?
    A: No, but you can access arrow functions via Fn + WASD. Takes some getting used to.

    Q: Are the switches on the G PRO X really swappable?
    A: Yes, but you need Logitech's proprietary GX switches. You can't use Cherry MX or other brands.

    Q: Which keyboard is better for programming?
    A: G PRO X, hands down. The F-keys and arrow keys are essential for coding.

    Q: Do both keyboards work on Mac?
    A: Yes, but some function keys may not work identically. The software experience is better on Windows.

    Q: Which has better build quality?
    A: Both are excellent. The G PRO X feels more solid due to weight, but the Huntsman Mini's aluminum construction is also premium.
